# Photosynthesis without the textbook fog

Plants do not make energy out of nothing. They use light to rearrange carbon dioxide and water into sugars that can be stored, moved, and spent later. Chlorophyll is important because it captures light, but the larger story is conversion and timing: leaves gather energy during the day, then the plant decides where that stored sugar is most useful.

Healthy explanations keep the mechanism concrete. Sunlight excites electrons, the cell uses that movement to build energy-rich molecules, and those molecules help assemble glucose. The result is not magic green paint. It is a quiet manufacturing process that keeps the plant alive and feeds the rest of the food web.
