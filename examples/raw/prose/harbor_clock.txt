# The harbor clock

At the edge of the old harbor there was a public clock that never matched any phone in town. It was usually four minutes slow, sometimes six, and once in midsummer it stopped entirely at 11:17 for three straight days. No one repaired it quickly because the clock had become part of the place. Fishermen glanced up at it, corrected for its mood, and kept walking.

Lena loved that small act of translation. The town still knew the real time, but the clock insisted on being interpreted rather than obeyed. It made the harbor feel older than it was and kinder than the schedule board beside it. Even the delays seemed less insulting under a clock that openly admitted it was imperfect.
